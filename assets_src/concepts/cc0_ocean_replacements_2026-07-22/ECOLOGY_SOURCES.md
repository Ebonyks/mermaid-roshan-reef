# Ecosystem context sources

These sources informed only the two context/placement boards. No external
image was supplied to image generation and no source pixels were copied.

## Caribbean shallow reef

- NOAA Fisheries, Staghorn Coral:
  https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/species/staghorn-coral/science
- NOAA Fisheries, Shallow Coral Reef Habitat:
  https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/national/habitat-conservation/shallow-coral-reef-habitat

The board uses simplified elkhorn/staghorn-like branching structure and massive
star/boulder-coral forms because these are characteristic Caribbean reef-building
families. Sea fans, tube sponges and seagrass provide independent supporting
silhouettes; the central authored route remains open.

## Norwegian coast and fjords

- Institute of Marine Research, Norwegian kelp forests:
  https://www.hi.no/hi/radgivning/marine-naturverdier-og-tiltak-i-kystsonen/marint-biologisk-mangfold/tareskog
- Institute of Marine Research, Norwegian coral occurrences:
  https://www.hi.no/hi/radgivning/marine-naturverdier-og-tiltak-i-kystsonen/marint-biologisk-mangfold/korallforekomster
- Institute of Marine Research, Norwegian coral reefs:
  https://www.hi.no/hi/temasider/hav-og-kyst/norske-korallrev

The board separates shallow kelp forest from deeper cold-water coral habitat.
The shallow silhouettes draw on tangle/stortare, sugar kelp, finger kelp and
winged kelp. The deep silhouettes draw on branching white cold-water reef coral,
horn-coral/sea-tree gardens and sponge gardens. These are density and habitat
references, not new Regen roles or combined runtime meshes.
